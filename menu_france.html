French Menu

The rich flavours of French cuisine are the result of a complex fusion of history, culture, tradition, and, most importantly, refined methods in the kitchen. Bouillabaisse, Quiche Lorraine, Boeuf Bourguignon, Escargots de Bourgogne, and Coq au Vin are just few of the French dishes that have propelled the country's cuisine to the forefront of international cuisine.

Appetizer

Onion Gallette

This rustic savory pie features an easy homemade crust and is filled to the brim with deeply caramelized onions and melty Gruyère cheese.

Boudin Noir Aux Pommes

A traditional French dish consisting of a blood sausage that is paired with apples. The French pride themselves on making an excellent blood sausage, so it is unsurprising that the dish makes for a flavorful starter throughout France, its popularity derived from the contrast between the acidity of the apples and the delicate flavors of the blood sausage.

Potatoes au Gratin

Tender potatoes are thinly sliced and smothered in cheese sauce before baking. Onion slices are also added to infuse light sweetness to the dish.

Salade Lyonnaise

A salad dish that consists of lettuce, bacon, croutons, and a poached egg that is placed on top of it.

Main Course

Beef Bourguignon

Beef stew braised in red wine, often red Burgundy, and beef stock, typically flavored with carrots, onions, garlic, and a bouquet garni, and garnished with pearl onions, mushrooms, and bacon.

Coq Au Vin

A classic French stew of chicken braised in red wine with mushrooms and crispy pancetta.

Bouillabaisse

A classic French fish soup with seafood. Use the freshest possible seafood for this recipe, and serve with a spicy sauce rouille for a memorable meal.

Chicken Cordon Bleu

Chicken stuffed with ham and cheese. Then, deep fried with coated crunchy golden breadcrumbs on top of it.

Dessert

Creme Brulee

Silky, creamy custard, topped with a thin, crackly layer of caramel. It’s a simple, yet elegant and decadent dessert.

Éclair

An éclair is a pastry made with choux dough filled with a cream and topped with a flavored icing. The dough, which is the same as that used for profiterole, is typically piped into an oblong shape with a pastry bag and baked until it is crisp and hollow inside.

Tarte Tatin

Tarte Tatin defies the cliché of fussy, flawless French desserts, while offering that same rich, sophisticated taste. Made with juicy apples doused in sweet caramel and draped with a buttery crust, it proves how simple ingredients can work magic together.

Macaron

A macaron is a sweet meringue-based confection made with egg white, icing sugar, granulated sugar, almond meal, and food colouring.
